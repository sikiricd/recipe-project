Pasta Salad

Description:

Pasta salad is a vibrant medley of flavors and textures, where al dente pasta intertwines with an array of fresh, colorful ingredients to create a refreshing and versatile dish. The star of the show is, of course, the pasta — whether it's rotini, fusilli, or penne, its firmness serves as the perfect canvas for absorbing the myriad tastes that accompany it. The pasta's hearty foundation is complemented by an assortment of crisp vegetables, such as cherry tomatoes bursting with sweetness or vibrant bell peppers that add a burst of color. But it's the dressing that ties this symphony of ingredients together. A zesty vinaigrette or creamy dressing envelops the pasta, coating each element with a harmonious blend of tanginess and savory richness. Optional additions like olives, feta cheese, or grilled chicken can elevate the complexity, offering bursts of brininess or protein-packed indulgence.

Pasta salad is not only a celebration of taste but also a testament to its adaptability — served chilled, it's a refreshing side dish on a hot summer day, and its vibrant hues make it a festive addition to any gathering. Whether enjoyed at a picnic, barbecue, or as a quick and satisfying meal, pasta salad is a culinary delight that promises a burst of flavors in every forkful.

Ingredients:

  • 1 Pound Tri-Colored Spiral Pasta
  • 1 (16 Oz.) Bottle of Italian-Style Salad Dressing
  • 6 Tbsp. Salad Seasoning Mix
  • 2 Cups Cherry Tomatoes (Diced)
  • 1 Green Bell Pepper (Chopped)
  • 1 Red Bell Pepper (Diced)
  • 1/2 Yellow Bell Pepper (Chopped)
  • 1 (2.25 Oz.) Can of Black Olives (Chopped)

Steps:

  1. Gather all ingredients.
  2. Bring a large pot of lightly salted water to a boil. Cook pasta in the boiling water, stirring occasionally, until tender yet firm to the bite (approximately 10-12 minutes). Rinse under cold water and drain.
  3. Whisk the dressing and salad seasoning mix together until smooth. Combine pasta, tomatoes, bell peppers, and olives in a salad bowl.
  4. Pour dressing over salad and toss to coat.
  5. Refrigerate salad (approximately eight (8) hours to overnight).
  6. Enjoy!
